**Vero PRD – Plan Feature & Rise‑and‑Shine Ritual**

---

# Overview

The **Plan feature** introduces a unified planning hub that replaces the current DeepWorkScreen1 tab in Vero’s bottom navigation.  When users tap **Plan**, a modal presents four ritual / review modules:

1. **Rise and Shine** – Morning ritual (existing functionality, enhanced)
2. **Shutdown** – Evening ritual & reflection
3. **Weekly Review** – Weekly planning (placeholder)
4. **Monthly Review** – Monthly reflection (placeholder)

This hub helps users organise their day, close their evenings, and maintain regular review cycles—all from one intuitive entry point—reinforcing mindful habit loops and streak‑based motivation.

# Core Features

## Plan Modal

* **What it does**: Presents a grid of planning options (Rise & Shine, Shutdown, Weekly Review, Monthly Review) in a bottom‑sheet modal launched from the *Plan* tab.
* **Why it's important**: Centralises rituals and planning workflows, reducing navigation friction and surfacing a clear mental model.
* **How it works**: When the *Plan* icon is tapped, a modal slides up containing a 2×2 grid of PlanOptionCards. Selecting a card routes the user to its respective ritual/review screen.

## Rise and Shine Ritual

* **Icon**: Sun or alarm clock
* **Purpose**: Morning routine and daily planning
* **Core Prompts**:

  1. What am I grateful for today?
  2. What am I avoiding?
  3. What am I excited about today?
  4. What is one thing I must accomplish today?
* **Timer Functionality**: 5‑ or 10‑minute Time‑Timer style countdown initiated by tapping the circular timer.
* **Progress Tracking**: On timer completion, the “Complete Ritual” button unlocks, the session is logged, and the streak counter updates.

## Shutdown Ritual

* **Icon**: Moon or power button
* **Purpose**: Evening routine and reflection

### 📌 Overview

The **Evening Ritual (Shutdown)** is a self‑guided journaling screen users launch from the *Plan* hub. It mirrors Rise & Shine’s structure, but focuses on end‑of‑day mindfulness, review, and closure. Vero only guides; journal entries stay in the user’s own medium.

### 🎯 Core Purpose

* Reinforce end‑of‑day mindfulness habits
* Help users meaningfully review their day
* Provide context (deep‑work, tasks, mood) to guide richer journaling
* Remain a lightweight ritual container (no in‑app diary storage in MVP)

### ✨ Feature Highlights

#### 1. Reflection Prompt Card (Default View)

* Shows all 4 default prompts at once (see **Core Prompts** below).
* Adds an optional 5th prompt when mood/energy trends warrant deeper reflection *(labelled “From your day…”)*.

#### 2. Dynamic Insight Card Switching (Top Section)

Tapping any insight button swaps the **top card** with contextual data:

* 🧠 **Deep Work Trends** – total focus time, timeline view, peak focus callouts.
* ✅ **Tasks Accomplished** – completed tasks list grouped by goal, upbeat summary.
* 💖 **Feeling Check‑Ins** – mood timeline sparkline, emotional tags, micro‑insight.

> Default state = Reflection Prompts card.

#### 3. Insight Buttons (Middle Strip)

Three toggle buttons displayed side‑by‑side: *Deep Work*, *Tasks Done*, *Feeling Check‑Ins*. Highlight shows active card; tapping again reverts to prompts.

#### 4. Time Timer (Bottom Section)

* Visual Time‑Timer countdown (default 5 min; user‑adjustable 5 / 10 min).
* Red wedge shrinks smoothly; prompt: “Take this time to journal your reflections.”

#### 5. Completion Flow

* CTA: **“I’m Done Journaling”** unlocks on timer finish.
* Triggers Shine radial glow + affirmation (e.g., “You honored your thoughts today.”).
* Post‑ritual summary card shows:

  * 🔥 Streak continuation status
  * 📊 Say/Do Ratio (task + goal tracking)
  * Encouraging insight line.

### Core Prompts

1. What am I grateful for today?
2. How can I improve?
3. What did I learn today?
4. Did I give my best today?

### Timer Functionality

5‑ or 10‑minute Time‑Timer countdown; mirrors Rise & Shine interaction.

### Progress Tracking

Session completion logs under *shutdown* ritual type; streak updates. Uses a cool calming colour palette.

### Implementation Notes

Shares component template with Rise & Shine (timer, toggle buttons, card container). Only prompt set, colour theme, and insight data differ.

### Suggested Visual Hierarchy & Sizing

* **Top Card** – \~40 % of screen height; displays prompts or insight stats.
* **Toggle Strip** – fixed 48 px height; equal‑width buttons with haptic feedback.
* **Time‑Timer** – \~30 % of screen height; dominant visual focus for countdown.
* **CTA Button** – 56–64 px height; full‑width, rounded corners.
* **Summary Overlay** – auto height; slides up post‑ritual, contains affirmation and metrics.

## Streak Tracking (Local) (Local)

* **What it does**: Records ritual completions and calculates consecutive‑day streaks per ritual type.
* **Why it's important**: Uses behavioural reinforcement to motivate consistent practice.
* **How it works**: On successful ritual completion, the date is persisted in `AsyncStorage`; streak counters are recalculated and surfaced in UI badges.

## Placeholder Reviews

* **What it does**: Shows disabled cards for Weekly and Monthly Review with “Coming Soon” messaging.
* **Why it's important**: Communicates product roadmap and gauges interest without over‑scoping MVP.
* **How it works**: PlanOptionCards are rendered with distinct opacity; taps trigger a toast indicating availability in a future update.

# User Experience

## Personas

* **Focused Founder** – busy entrepreneur needing morning clarity & nightly shut‑down.
* **Mindful Maker** – creative who values ritualised reflection.
* **Busy Parent** – juggles commitments; needs simple, guided planning blocks.

## Key Flows

1. **Launch Plan Hub**
   ‑ User taps *Plan* icon in bottom nav → modal slides up with 4 cards.
2. **Morning Ritual (Rise and Shine)**
   ‑ Tap card → ritual screen shows title, tappable timer, questions, disabled CTA.
   ‑ Timer completes (5 min) → haptic; CTA enabled → tap → ritual logged → streak++ → navigates to deepWorkScreen2.
3. **Evening Ritual (Shutdown)**
   ‑ Similar flow with evening prompts & cooldown palette.
4. **Placeholder Reviews**
   ‑ Tapping shows “Coming Soon” toast / disabled overlay.

## UI/UX Considerations

* Modal grid 2×2 with large icons, titles, micro‑copy; swipe down or tap outside to dismiss.
* Colour cues: warm energising for Rise & Shine, cool calming for Shutdown.
* Smooth spring animations; haptic on option select and ritual completion.
* Accessibility: 44 px touch targets, VoiceOver labels.

# Technical Architecture

## System Components

* **BottomTabNavigator**: replaces old *DeepWorkScreen1* entry with *Plan*.
* **PlanModal**: React‑Native Modal rendering grid of PlanOptionCards.
* **PlanOptionCard**: reusable card component with icon, title, state (active/disabled).
* **RiseAndShineScreen** (`Screen1.js` + `FocusTimer`, `QuestionsCard`, `PrimaryButton`).
* **ShutdownScreen** (reuse Rise template; inject prompt set + theme props).
* **StreakManager**: local storage util (per‑ritual streak + global stats).

## APIs / Integrations

* **Local MVP**: `AsyncStorage` only.
* **Future**: Firebase / MongoDB sync for multi‑device continuity.

## Infrastructure Requirements

* Expo SDK (RN 0.79 / Expo 53), `react‑native‑svg`, `expo‑haptics`, `@react‑navigation` modals.
* No backend infra for Phase 1.

# Risks & Mitigations

## Performance of SVG Timer

* **Risk**: Intensive SVG arc animation may cause jank on low‑end devices.
* **Impact**: Degraded user experience and lower engagement.
* **Mitigation**: Use lightweight `react‑native‑svg`, throttle frame updates, and profile with Flipper.

## Scope Creep into Weekly / Monthly Review

* **Risk**: Expanding review features too early.
* **Impact**: Delays MVP delivery and dilutes focus.
* **Mitigation**: Keep Weekly / Monthly cards as “Coming Soon” placeholders; enforce feature flags for any new work.

## AsyncStorage Corruption / Data Loss

* **Risk**: Local data store may be cleared or corrupted by OS or app updates.
* **Impact**: Streaks reset; user trust erodes.
* **Mitigation**: Implement defensive reads/writes, add unit tests, and introduce optional cloud backup in Phase 3.

## Resource Constraints

* **Risk**: Limited engineering bandwidth and potential knowledge gaps with React Native + Expo.
* **Impact**: Slower development and rushed QA.
* **Mitigation**: Prioritise MVP scope, reuse shared components, and ship in small atomic pull requests.
